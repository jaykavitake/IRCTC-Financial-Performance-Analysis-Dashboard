# IRCTC-Financial-Performance-Analysis-Dashboard
An interactive Excel dashboard analyzing IRCTC's financial performance from 2015 to 2025, built as part of my Data Analytics learning journey.


# Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive financial performance analysis of IRCTC (Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation) — one of India's most prominent public sector enterprises — spanning 10 years (2015–2025).

The dashboard was built entirely in Microsoft Excel using Pivot Tables, dynamic charts, slicers, and KPI cards. It is designed to give a clear, at-a-glance view of IRCTC's revenue growth, profitability trends, operational efficiency, and dividend behavior over the years.

This project marks a significant milestone in my Data Analytics journey, demonstrating skills in data cleaning, financial analysis, and dashboard design.

# Dataset Description
The dataset covers IRCTC's annual financial statements with the following key fields:
FieldDescriptionYearFinancial year (Mar-2015 to Mar-2025), RevenueTotal revenue earned (in Crores), ExpensesTotal expenses incurred (in Crores), Net ProfitProfit after tax (in Crores), Other IncomeNon-operating income (in Crores), EPSEarnings Per Share , Dividend % Dividend payout percentage , OPM % Operating Profit Margin percentage , Tax % Effective tax rate percentage

# Dashboard Features

 Dynamic Slicers — Filter data by year (Mar-2019 to Mar-2022 and beyond).

 KPI Summary Cards — Instant view of Total Revenue, Total Expenses, Highest Net Profit, and Total Other Income.

 Interactive Charts — All visuals update dynamically based on slicer selection.

 Multi-sheet Structure — Organized into DATA, PIVOT_TABLE1, KPI's, DASHBOARD, and Colours tabs.

 Clean Layout — Professional dark-navy colour theme for readability and visual impact.


# Charts & Visualizations

1. Top 5 Revenue and Expense Years (Clustered Bar Chart)
   
Highlights the five best-performing years by revenue alongside corresponding profit figures. Clearly shows IRCTC's consistent upward trajectory from 2020 to 2025.

2.  Revenue vs. Expense Trend (Line Chart)

A dual-line chart tracking sales and expenses from 2015 to 2025. Reveals how post-COVID recovery (2022 onwards) dramatically accelerated the revenue-expense gap in IRCTC's favour.

3.  OPM% By Year (Line Chart)
   
Operating Profit Margin trend over 10 years. Peaked at 47% in 2022, followed by stabilization around 33–36%, reflecting maturing operational efficiency.

4.  Net Profit By Year (Bar Chart)
   
Shows IRCTC's net profit growth from ₹131 Cr (2015) to ₹1,315 Cr (2025) — approximately a 10x increase over a decade.

5.  EPS vs. Dividend Payout Trend (Combo Chart)
   
Compares Earnings Per Share (bar) against Dividend Payout % (line). The spike in EPS in 2016 (18.86) and the high dividend payout of 98% in 2019 are notable highlights.

6.  Other Income By Year (Horizontal Bar Chart)
    
Breaks down non-operating income year-wise. 2023 saw the highest at ₹148 Cr, while 2025 recorded the lowest at ₹276 Cr on a cumulative filtered basis.

7.  Tax% By Year (Area Chart)
    
Effective tax rate trend from 39% (2015–2016) steadily declining to 25% (2025), reflecting the impact of India's corporate tax reforms and improved tax planning.


##  Business Questions Answered

This dashboard was built to answer the following key business questions:

1. How has IRCTC's revenue and profit grown over the last 10 years?
   
2. Which years recorded the highest and lowest net profit?
   
3. How did COVID-19 impact IRCTC's financial performance?
 
4. Is IRCTC managing its expenses efficiently as revenue scales?
   
5. How has the Operating Profit Margin (OPM%) trended over time?
    
6. What is the relationship between EPS and Dividend payout behavior?
    
7. How has the effective tax rate changed after India's 2019 corporate tax reforms?
   
8. Which years contributed the most to other (non-operating) income?


# Insights & Findings

1.Decade-long Growth: IRCTC's net profit grew nearly 10x from ₹131 Cr in 2015 to ₹1,315 Cr in 2025, reflecting strong business expansion.

2.COVID Impact Visible: A sharp dip in revenue and profit is clearly visible around 2020–2021, followed by one of the strongest recoveries among Indian PSUs.

3.Improving Margins: OPM% peaked at 47% post-COVID (2022) due to lean cost management during the recovery phase before normalizing to ~33%.

4.Declining Tax Rate: Effective tax rate dropped from 39% to 25%, aligned with India's 2019 corporate tax rate cut benefiting listed companies.

5.Generous Dividend Policy: IRCTC paid out 98% of earnings as dividends in 2019, making it one of the most shareholder-friendly PSUs in India.

6.Expense Management: Despite revenue scaling significantly, expenses have grown at a slower rate, pointing to strong operational leverage.
